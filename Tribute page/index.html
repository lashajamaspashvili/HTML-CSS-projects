Andreas Nikolaus Lauda

Three-time F1 World Drivers' Champion

Niki Lauda is driving Ferrari's car.

Here's a time line of Niki Lauda's life:

  • 1949 Born on 22 February in Vienna, Austria
  • 1970 One race in World Sportscar Championship
  • 1971 10th place in European Formula Two championship, one race(debut) in Formula One, in STP March Racing Team
  • 1972 5th place in European Formula Two championship, 11 race without winning in Formula One, champion of British Formula Two
  • 1973 7th place in European Touring Car Championship, with BMW Alpina, one race in World Sportscar Championship, 18th place in Formula One championship, with Marlboro-BRM
  • 1974 He moved to Scuderia Ferrari and took 4th place in Formula One championship, he also had one single race in World Sportscar Championship
  • 1975 Formula One Champion
  • 1976 2nd place after James Hunt, in this year there was his famous accident, 1976 German Grand Prix in Nürburgring, he was burned and sensationly survived, after this accident, next 43 years he had several burns and scars
  • 1977 Formula One Champion
  • 1978 He moved to Parmalat Racing Team, 4th place in championship
  • 1979 14th place in championship and he left Formula One, he won BMW M1 Procar Championship
  • 1982 Niki Lauda returned in Formula One and took 5th place with Marlboro McLaren International
  • 1983 10th place in championship
  • 1984 1st place, Lauda becomes Three-time world champion of Formula One
  • 1985 He took 10th place in his last season in Formula One
  • 1993 Lauda returned to Formula One in a managerial position when Luca di Montezemolo offered him a consulting role at Ferrari. Halfway through the 2001 season, Lauda assumed the role of team principal of the Jaguar Formula One team. The team, however, failed to improve and Lauda was made redundant, together with 70 other key figures, at the end of 2002.
  • 2012 In September 2012 he was appointed non-executive chairman of the Mercedes AMG Petronas F1 Team. He took part in negotiations to sign Lewis Hamilton to a three-year deal with Mercedes in 2013.
  • 2013 The 1976 F1 battle between Niki Lauda and James Hunt was dramatized in the film Rush (2013), where Lauda was played by Daniel Brühl. Lauda made a cameo appearance at the end of the film. Lauda said of Hunt's death, "When I heard he'd died age 45 of a heart attack I wasn't surprised, I was just sad." He also said that Hunt was one of the very few he liked, a smaller number of people he respected and the only person he had envied.
  • 2019 Died in his sleep on 20 May of 2019, aged 70, at the University Hospital of Zürich.
